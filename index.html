Walter Elias "Walt" Disney

Co-founder of Walt Disney Productions and founder of theme parks Disneyland and Walt Disney world

Timeline of Walt Disney

  • 1901 - Born in the Hermosa section of Chicago, Illinois
  • 1911 - his family moved to Kansas City, where Disney developed a love for trains
  • 1918 - joined the Red Cross and was sent to France for a year to drive an ambulance to help with the war effort
  • 1919 - moved to Kansas City and worked as an apprentice artist at the Pesmen-Rubin Commercial Art Studio, where he drew commercial illustrations for advertising, theater programs and catalogs, and befriended fellow artist Ub Iwerks
  • 1920 - Disney and Iwerks started their own business, the short-lived Iwerks-Disney Commercial Artists
  • 1921 - Disney opened a new business with a co-worker from the Film Ad Co, Fred Harman, success led to the establishment of Laugh-O-Gram Studio
  • 1923 - Moved to LA and signed a contract for six Alice comedies, with an option for two further series of six episodes each. This lead to Disney and his brother Roy forming the Disney Brothers Studio‍—which later became The Walt Disney Company‍—to produce the films
  • 1925 - Disney hired an ink artist, Lillian Bounds. They married in July of that year, at her brother's house in her hometown of Lewiston, Idaho.
  • 1927 - Disney and Iwerks developed Mickey Mouse, possibly inspired by a pet mouse that Disney had adopted while working in his Laugh-O-Gram studio, although the origins of the character are unclear
  • 1928 - Disney used synchronized sound on the third short, Steamboat Willie, to create the first post-produced sound cartoon. After the animation was complete, Disney signed a contract with the former executive of Universal Pictures, Pat Powers, to use the "Powers Cinephone" recording system;[61] Cinephone became the new distributor for Disney's early sound cartoons, which soon became popular.
